Week Eight Recap

29 June 2015

Here's a list of the topics covered this week:

  • Backbone JS - We explored the MV structure that Backbone helps developers create in a bit more depth.
  • Node JS - Our instructor gave us a crash course, and we jumped right into our first node project.
  • C3 JS - An awesome charting library built on top of D3 JS that we used to visualize our data.

At first glance this week seems a lot lighter than weeks six and seven. Don't let the short list fool you. Node and Backbone have steep learning curves. I certainly have not learned their intricacies in one week.

I think our class is starting to show the wear and tear of eight long weeks of heavy lectures. Everyone seems less bright eyed and more tired at the sight of each new tool. I can definitely understand the feeling. The new material is very interesting and I really want to learn it all. But as we move closer to the end of the program, I find myself wanting to really solidify my skills in the essential learning categories as laid out by our instructor. That being said, I am exploring each new topic as we go through the program. I have done a pretty good job not going back to old topics even when that's what I want to do.

An Aside on Essential Skills

What does essential learning mean? The topics that are deemed essential by our instructor are the ones the every junior developer must know to be employable. Here's an overview:

  • HTML & CSS
  • Vanilla JS
  • Jquery
  • AJAX
  • A CSS Framework
  • A JS Framework

This is by know means an exhaustive list. These are the topics that a junior developer must be strong in to succeed in the job search. Keep in mind that this is a the absolute minimum according to our instructor.
