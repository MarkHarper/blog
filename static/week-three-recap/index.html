Week Five Recap

09 June 2015

One more week down at TIY and our workflow has changed so much in only seven days. Here is a quick rundown of the tools we added to our arsenal this week:

  • Backbone JsSo far we have mostly used the routing function
  • Gulp Js - a streaming build system that really speeds up the development process.
  • Visual Studio Code - I know. It's just a code editor, but any code editor that offers git commands in the UI is worth checking out.
  • SASS/SCSS - Syntactically awesome style sheets!! Gotta love variables and ways to group CSS settings.
  • CSS Frameworks - We had an overview of several options, but a live demo of Twitter's Bootstrap. Here are a few.
    • Bootstrap
    • Bourbon - Even without considering the name, it seemed like the most interesting choice to me.
    • Foundation


Wow, that really was a lot. This week really pushed us forward in terms of intelligently structuring our projects and planning ahead. This seems like a great lead in to more work with JS frameworks like Backone and Angular. Especially because of their focus on the separation of concerns and the planning required in that.

Takeaways

I really only have two takeaways this week.
-Don't research too much : In planning my project, I spent a day reading and researching Backbone JS. Ultimately, I only used bits and pieces of what I learned. The wasted time also cost me a lot of valuable time. Don't do what I did. Follow the process. Do some planning/wireframing/diagramming, then proceed to building your project.

-So much to learn, so little time : I love learning about all of these useful technologies, but I like to feel confident using a tool before I move on to learn something else. But the more time I spend coding, the more I am becoming used to being uncomfortable.

Irritation of the Week

There was no big bad irritation this week. I am becoming acclimated to the struggle with new tools.

Resources:

  • Backbone Fundamentals - an excellent intro to backbone with example projects to work through

  • Chartist JS - a javascript library for building stylish and responsive charts. This wasn't part of the class work, but I thought it would be cool to restructure an earlier project's statistics on the Titanic Tragedy with data visualization.

Fascinating Tech News:

  • Swift Open Source - The title says it all. Apple is releasing its iOS language as an open source development project.

Dream Destination of the Week

When I outlined this recap, I didn't have any specific place in mind. I simply wrote "somewhere warm and beautiful". In light of that mindset, I am letting google do the choosing this week. And the choice is...

the Pacific island of Saipan.

Enjoy the trip!! I will be coding away. At least until I finish my program. Maybe a brief trip before the job search will be possible.
